About Wasaka Robotic Team

Wasaka Robotic Team is a student organization dedicated to building robots and creating meaningful learning experiences in robotics, engineering, and technology.

Wasaka Robotic Team is a student organization focused on robot research and development. Our activities range from national robotics competitions to internal research projects that aim to bring technology closer to society.

The team welcomes students from different backgrounds and skill levels. We learn robotics from the ground up: electronics, programming, mechanics, and artificial intelligence. Regular practice sessions, design reviews, and knowledge-sharing meetups are part of our weekly rhythm.

Vision

To become an excellent, innovative, and sustainable robotics team that can compete at national and international levels.

Mission
  • Build a strong learning and research culture in robotics.
  • Develop reliable robots for competitions and real-world applications.
  • Prepare students to innovate in technology and industry.
  • Create networks with other teams, communities, and industry partners.

Divisions

Our team is organized into divisions that focus on different types of robots and skill sets. Members can specialize or explore multiple areas.

KRSRI Team

Focused on the Indonesian Search and Rescue Robot Contest (KRSRI), this team develops robots that navigate challenging arenas, detect victims, and complete mission scenarios.

KRSBI Team

Dedicated on the Indonesian Humanoid Soccer Robot Contest (KRSBI Humanoid), this team develops autonomous humanoid robots capable of playing soccer, navigating the field, maintaining balance, and executing strategic movements during competitive match scenarios.

Racing Plane Team

Focused on the Racing Plane division of the Indonesian Flying Robotics Contest (KRTI), this team develops high-speed unmanned aircraft designed for optimal aerodynamics, agile maneuvering, and precise autonomous or remote-controlled flight to compete in time-based racing challenges.

Each division collaborates and shares knowledge so members can explore different areas of robotics while still contributing to one unified team.

Hexapod Robot

Hexayos

A six-legged robot built to walk stably on different terrains. It serves as a research platform for motion control, inverse kinematics, and physics-based simulations.

  • 6 legs with 18 degrees of freedom.
  • Powered by a microcontroller and mini PC.
  • Supports simulation in virtual environments.
Robot Visual
5-DoF Robotic Arm

Wasaka-Arm

A 5-DoF robotic manipulator designed for precision tasks and object handling. It provides stable motion, accurate positioning, and flexible movement for a wide range of operations.

  • 5 degrees of freedom for versatile articulation.
  • High-torque servo motors for stable control.
  • Capable of performing pick-and-place operations.
Robot Visual
KRTMI Robot

Wasaka-Sorter

A smart waste-sorting robot developed for the KRTMI 2024 competition. It identifies, classifies, and separates various types of waste autonomously using sensors and machine learning.

  • Object detection and classification with vision sensors.
  • Automated waste-sorting mechanism.
  • Designed for high accuracy and eco-friendly waste handling.

Join Us

Wasaka Robotic Team is always open to students who want to learn and grow in robotics, whether they are complete beginners or already experienced.

Who Can Join?

  • Students from any study program who are interested in technology.
  • Willing to spend time on practice, research, and competition preparation.
  • Ready to learn new things and work in a team environment.
  • No need to be an expert; a strong learning attitude is more important.

How to Apply

  • Fill out the online registration form (link will be opened during recruitment periods).
  • Join an introduction session about the team and its divisions.
  • Complete a simple task based on your interest: mechanical, electrical, software, or documentation.
  • Attend a short interview to discuss motivation and commitment.
